ARTICLE 3: TERM AND TERMINATION

3.1 Initial Term: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of thirty-six (36) months.

3.2 Renewal: The Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the current term.

3.3 Termination for Convenience: Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon one hundred eighty (180) days prior written notice.

3.4 Termination for Cause: Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
   a) The other party commits a material breach and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice
   b) The other party becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy
   c) The other party is found to be in violation of applicable laws or regulations

3.5 Early Termination Fees: If Client terminates for convenience before completion of the Initial Term:
   a) Within Year 1: Client shall pay 50% of remaining contract value
   b) Within Year 2: Client shall pay 35% of remaining contract value
   c) Within Year 3: Client shall pay 20% of remaining contract value

3.6 Upon termination, Provider shall:
   a) Return all Client data within thirty (30) days in a standard format
   b) Provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days
   c) Delete all Client data from Provider systems within ninety (90) days

ARTICLE 4: DATA SECURITY AND PRIVACY

4.1 The Provider acknowledges that it will have access to Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under HIPAA.

4.2 Provider agrees to:
   a) Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards as required by HIPAA
   b) Encrypt all data in transit and at rest using AES-256 encryption
   c) Conduct annual third-party security audits
   d) Maintain SOC 2 Type II certification
   e) Report any security incident within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery

4.3 Data Breach Liability: In the event of a data breach caused by Provider's negligence:
   a) Provider shall bear all costs of notification to affected individuals
   b) Provider shall provide two (2) years of credit monitoring to affected individuals
   c) Provider shall be liable for regulatory fines up to a maximum of $5,000,000
   d) Provider shall indemnify Client against third-party claims arising from the breach

4.4 Data Ownership: All Client data remains the sole property of Client. Provider acquires no rights to Client data except as necessary to perform services.

ARTICLE 5: SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

5.1 System Availability: Provider guarantees 99.9% uptime, measured monthly.

5.2 SLA Credits: If uptime falls below guaranteed levels:
   a) 99.0% - 99.9%: 10% credit on monthly fee
   b) 98.0% - 99.0%: 25% credit on monthly fee
   c) Below 98.0%: 50% credit on monthly fee

5.3 Support Response Times:
   a) Critical Issues (system down): Response within 15 minutes, resolution within 4 hours
   b) High Priority: Response within 1 hour, resolution within 8 hours
   c) Medium Priority: Response within 4 hours, resolution within 24 hours
   d) Low Priority: Response within 24 hours, resolution within 72 hours

5.4 Scheduled Maintenance: Provider shall perform maintenance during off-peak hours (2:00 AM - 6:00 AM EST) with at least 72 hours advance notice.

ARTICLE 6: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 Pre-Existing IP: Each party retains ownership of intellectual property owned prior to this Agreement.

6.2 Custom Developments: Any custom software, configurations, or integrations developed specifically for Client shall be owned by Client.

6.3 Provider Platform: The underlying EHR platform, including all source code, algorithms, and architecture, remains the exclusive property of Provider.

6.4 License Grant: Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Provider's platform for the duration of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 7: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Limitation of Liability: EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY, DATA SECURITY OBLIGATIONS, OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, NEITHER PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

7.2 Exclusion of Damages: NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES.

7.3 Provider Indemnification: Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from:

7.4 Client Indemnification: Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Provider from:

ARTICLE 8: CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 Definition: "Confidential Information" includes all non-public information disclosed by either party, including but not limited to: business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical specifications, and trade secrets.

8.2 Obligations: The receiving party shall:
   a) Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement
   b) Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care as its own
   c) Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent
   d) Return or destroy Confidential Information upon request or termination

8.3 Exceptions: Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
   a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party
   b) Was rightfully in the receiving party's possession prior to disclosure
   c) Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
   d) Is required to be disclosed by law (with prompt notice to disclosing party)

8.4 Duration: Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for five (5) years.
